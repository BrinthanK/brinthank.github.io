Presently, I am pursuing my master's degree (major component of research) at the University of Moratuwa. My research primarily centres on material separation and characterisation, where I study meaningful approaches integrating material characterisation techniques with Artificial Intelligence (AI) methodologies. Previously, I earned my undergraduate degree in Earth Resources Engineering from the same university, graduating with a BSc (Honours).

My research interests revolve around the intricate characterisation of microstructures within 3D materials, aimed at broadening their applications and enhancing our comprehension of these substances. Furthermore, I am engaged in exploring material characterisation through multimodal imaging and computer vision, with a special emphasis on computational X-ray microanalysis and its underlying complexities.

Sustainability and decarbonisation serve as fundamental principles guiding my research endeavours. Every facet of my research work is scrutinised through the lens of these principles. Additionally, I actively explore advanced machine learning and deep learning methodologies, envisioning their potential applications in microscopy and spectroscopy.
